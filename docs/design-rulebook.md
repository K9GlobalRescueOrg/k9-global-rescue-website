# K9GR Design Rulebook

This rulebook adapts the installed Taste Skill approach to K9 Global Rescue. It should guide future design reviews and frontend edits before generic design-taste rules are applied.

## Brand Personality

K9 Global Rescue should feel serious, credible, operational, global, rescue-focused, and donor-worthy. The site should signal field work, partner coordination, urgent rescue needs, and accountable support. It should not feel like a generic nonprofit template, a cute pet brand, a pastel charity site, or a corporate SaaS landing page.

## Color Rules

- Keep black and off-black as the dominant base.
- Use official-logo gold as the primary accent.
- Use ivory/tan for warmth only when contrast stays strong.
- Gold should guide attention: CTAs, focus states, borders, small labels, and selected section emphasis.
- Avoid overusing gold as a large background or decorative wash.
- Avoid low-contrast gold-on-tan, muted text on black, and unreadable secondary buttons.
- Never add AI-purple gradients, neon glows, random blue/purple mesh, or pastel charity palettes.

## Typography Rules

- Use strong, legible sans-serif typography with a serious operational tone.
- Headlines should be short, direct, and readable at mobile sizes.
- Keep body copy concise and around 65 characters per line where possible.
- Avoid long text walls, all-caps overload, tiny labels, and decorative serif flourishes.
- Use type hierarchy to create authority before adding visual effects.

## Layout Rules

- Favor strong left alignment, clear hierarchy, and purposeful spacing.
- Keep the first viewport focused on identity, work, and actions.
- Use grids only when the content warrants comparison or scanning.
- Do not repeat the same card-grid section pattern on every page.
- Avoid broad structural rewrites unless the redesign scope is approved first.

## Homepage Rules

- The homepage must immediately communicate who K9GR is, what it does, and how to help.
- Donate, Volunteer, and Learn About Our Work or equivalent operations links must remain visible.
- Rescue stories should only present verified stories or careful neutral placeholders that do not pretend to be full stories.
- Avoid fake stats, fake partner logos, fake dashboards, and generic impact blocks.

## CTA Rules

- Primary CTA language should be short: Donate, Volunteer, Apply to Volunteer, Contact, Learn About Our Work.
- Keep CTA intent consistent across header, hero, content sections, and footer.
- Buttons must have strong contrast, clear hover/focus states, and mobile-safe text wrapping.
- Donation links must never point to invented processors, fake campaigns, or placeholder payment flows.

## Image Rules

- Prefer K9GR-approved field, rescue, team, transport, or partner photography.
- Temporary images must be sober and relevant.
- Avoid generic smiling stock-photo nonprofit imagery, cute pet lifestyle shots, or staged corporate volunteer imagery.
- Do not reuse the same stock dog image to imply multiple different rescue stories.
- Meaningful images need useful alt text.

## Navigation Rules

- Keep navigation scannable and operational.
- Donate must remain visible.
- Desktop navigation should fit on one line.
- Mobile navigation should be clear, compact, and easy to operate.
- Add current-page treatment in a future polish pass.

## Card And Section Rules

- Cards are for repeated content, framed tools, team members, stories, or program indexes.
- Do not place cards inside cards.
- Avoid three identical feature cards unless there is a real content reason.
- Avoid decorative status dots, fake metrics, unnecessary badges, and dashboard motifs.
- Quote sections should be used sparingly so the belief statement remains high-impact.

## Copy Rules

- Copy should be direct, specific, and calm.
- Avoid wordy, cheesy, or generic impact filler.
- Do not add fake claims, fake stats, fake partners, fake donation links, or fake rescue stories.
- Public UI must not include placeholder, review, verify, approval, or pending language.
- Crisis, military, international, and donation copy should avoid overpromising.

## Mobile Rules

- Mobile layouts should feel deliberately composed, not just collapsed.
- Buttons need comfortable tap targets and no cramped wrapping.
- Header and hero should not consume excessive vertical space.
- Long team bios, forms, and lists need readable spacing.
- No information should depend on hover.

## Accessibility And Readability Rules

- Maintain WCAG AA contrast for text and controls.
- Keep visible keyboard focus states.
- Use semantic landmarks and labels.
- Form labels stay above fields; placeholders never replace labels.
- Avoid low-contrast black/gold combinations.
- Keep line lengths, font sizes, and section spacing comfortable on mobile and desktop.

## Anti-Slop Rules

- No generic AI-purple gradients.
- No random glassmorphism.
- No fake dashboards.
- No three identical feature cards unless there is a real content reason.
- No decorative status dots.
- No meaningless "impact" statements.
- No fake statistics.
- No generic smiling stock-photo nonprofit look.
- No pastel charity template aesthetic.
- No long text walls.
- No public placeholder, review, approval, verify, or pending language.
- No unnecessary animations.
- No overuse of gold.
- No unreadable low-contrast black/gold combinations.
