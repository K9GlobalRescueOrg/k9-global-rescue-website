# K9GR Design Skill

Use this skill for K9 Global Rescue website design review, frontend polish, copy review, and future page work. It adapts Taste Skill's anti-slop discipline to this specific brand.

## Design Read

K9 Global Rescue is a serious, operational, global rescue nonprofit. The site should feel credible, direct, field-aware, donor-worthy, and anchored in the official black-and-gold logo. It must not drift into generic nonprofit softness, pastel charity templates, fake impact marketing, overly corporate polish, or AI-generated visual tricks.

## Core Rules

- Preserve the black and gold brand direction from the official logo.
- Use gold as a controlled signal color, not a flood fill.
- Keep copy direct, specific, and short.
- Keep Donate, Volunteer, and Learn About Our Work or equivalent work-learning actions obvious.
- Do not invent claims, partners, rescue results, donation URLs, stories, numbers, or operational promises.
- Do not expose placeholder, review, verify, pending, launch approval, or build/deploy language in public UI.
- Work in small, reviewable improvements. Do not rewrite the whole site without explicit approval.

## Brand Personality

- Serious, credible, operational, global, rescue-focused, donor-worthy.
- Field support and partner coordination, not cute pet adoption marketing.
- Warm enough to care about individual animals, but never sentimental filler.
- Confident, plainspoken, and accountable.

## Color Rules

- Primary base: black and off-black.
- Primary accent: K9 gold.
- Supporting text: warm ivory and muted tan only when contrast stays strong.
- Gold should highlight actions, focus states, section edges, and key labels.
- Avoid gold-on-tan or low-contrast black/gold combinations.
- Avoid generic AI-purple gradients, random neon, pastel charity colors, and one-note gold saturation.

## Typography Rules

- Use strong, legible sans-serif typography that feels operational and mature.
- Headlines should be compact and forceful, usually sentence case or restrained title case.
- Body copy should stay around 65 characters per line where possible.
- Avoid long text walls, excessive uppercase blocks, and tiny low-contrast text.
- Do not introduce decorative serif emphasis unless the brand direction explicitly changes.

## Layout Rules

- Favor strong left alignment, clear hierarchy, and purposeful section sequencing.
- Use cards only for repeated items or framed tools; do not make every section a card.
- Avoid repeating the same three-card/four-card grid pattern across every page.
- Use section contrast through spacing, type, and content structure before adding decoration.
- Keep hero CTAs visible and easy to understand on first view.

## Homepage Rules

- The homepage must immediately answer: who K9GR is, what work it does, and how to help.
- Donate and Volunteer must remain primary actions.
- Learn About Our Work should lead to operations or program detail.
- Rescue stories should only use verified story content or clearly neutral labels.
- Do not use fake stats, fake partner logos, fake dashboards, or generic impact claims.

## CTA Rules

- Primary CTAs: Donate, Volunteer, Contact, Apply to Volunteer, Learn About Our Work.
- Keep CTA labels short and consistent across pages.
- Button text must fit cleanly on mobile and desktop.
- Primary buttons need strong contrast; secondary buttons need visible borders and text.
- Do not point donation CTAs to fake or placeholder payment destinations.

## Image Rules

- Prefer real K9GR-approved photography when available.
- If temporary images are used, they must be sober, relevant, and not generic smiling nonprofit stock.
- Avoid cute, soft, pastel, staged, or emotionally manipulative imagery.
- Avoid using the same stock dog image as a stand-in for multiple different rescue stories.
- All meaningful images need accurate alt text.

## Navigation Rules

- Keep the global nav scannable and operational.
- Donate must stay visible.
- Desktop nav should fit on one line.
- Mobile nav should be easy to open, read, and close without layout jump.
- Use active/current-page treatment in a future polish pass.

## Card And Section Rules

- Cards need a real content reason.
- Avoid three identical feature cards unless the content truly benefits from direct comparison.
- Avoid decorative status dots, meaningless badges, and dashboard-like metrics.
- Vary repeated page structures: split section, list, quote, program index, story index, or contact path.
- Keep QuoteBand high-impact by using it sparingly.

## Copy Rules

- Direct, concrete, and nonprofit-credible.
- No fake statistics, fake urgency, fake partner proof, fake donation links, or unverified rescue stories.
- Avoid "impact" filler and generic phrases like "making a difference" without specifics.
- Do not publish internal review, placeholder, pending, approval, or verification language.
- Use careful wording for active crisis or military/international work.

## Mobile Rules

- Single-column layouts must feel intentional, not merely collapsed.
- CTAs need comfortable tap targets and no awkward wrapping.
- Header/logo/nav should not consume too much vertical space.
- Long team bios and story lists should remain readable with clear spacing.
- Avoid hover-only information.

## Accessibility And Readability

- Maintain WCAG AA contrast for text and buttons.
- Preserve keyboard focus states.
- Use semantic HTML.
- Keep labels above form fields.
- Avoid placeholder-as-label.
- Ensure external links are intentional and marked with safe `rel` values.

## Things To Avoid

- No generic AI-purple gradients.
- No random glassmorphism.
- No fake dashboards.
- No three identical feature cards unless there is a real content reason.
- No decorative status dots.
- No meaningless "impact" statements.
- No fake statistics.
- No generic smiling stock-photo nonprofit look.
- No pastel charity template aesthetic.
- No long text walls.
- No public placeholder, review, approval, verify, or pending language.
- No unnecessary animations.
- No overuse of gold.
- No unreadable low-contrast black/gold combinations.
